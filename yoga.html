Yoga(Asanas)

Tadasana (Mountain Pose)

Stand with feet together, arms relaxed by the sides, and palms facing forward. Keep the body aligned from heels to the crown of the head, engaging the thighs and lifting the chest.

Benefits

Improves posture, strengthens thighs and knees, tones abdomen, and helps with focus and concentration.

Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward-Facing Dog Pose)

Begin on your hands and knees, with hands slightly ahead of the shoulders and knees below the hips. Lift the hips upward, straightening the arms and legs, forming an inverted V-shape. Press the hands into the mat, lengthen the spine, and relax the head between the arms.

Benefits

Stretches the shoulders, hamstrings, calves, and hands. Strengthens arms and legs, relieves mild depression, and energizes the body.

Uttanasana (Standing Forward Bend)

Stand with feet hip-width apart, hinge at the hips, and fold forward, bringing the chest towards the thighs. Keep the knees slightly bent if necessary, and let the head hang heavy.

Benefits

Stretches the hips, hamstrings, and calves. Calms the mind, relieves stress, and improves digestion.

Virabhadrasana I (Warrior I Pose)

Start in a standing position, step one foot back, keeping it at a 45-degree angle. Bend the front knee to a 90-degree angle, keeping the knee over the ankle. Raise the arms overhead, palms facing each other, and gaze forward.

Benefits

Strengthens legs, arms, shoulders, and back. Improves focus, balance, and stability.
